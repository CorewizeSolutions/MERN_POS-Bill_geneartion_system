4.5.4 Process Flow Explanation
Here‚Äôs how the Cart Page works step-by-step in our POS system:
1.	Adding Items to Cart
      ‚Ä¢	Items are selected from the homepage and automatically appear in the Cart Page with their default quantity, price, and stored GST %.  
2.	Reviewing Cart Items
      ‚Ä¢	The cashier can review each item‚Äôs details: name, quantity, price, tax %, and calculated amount.
3.	Modifying Quantities
      ‚Ä¢	Clicking the ‚Äú+‚Äù increases quantity, clicking the ‚Äú‚Äì‚Äù decreases quantity (but not below 1).
      ‚Ä¢	Every change triggers a real-time recalculation of subtotal, tax, and total.
4.	Removing Items
      ‚Ä¢	The delete icon removes an item instantly, updating totals and tax amounts accordingly.
5.	Applying Discounts
      ‚Ä¢	The cashier can enter a discount % (e.g., 30%).	
      ‚Ä¢	The system recalculates the base price, applies the discount, then adds GST on the discounted price.
6.	GST Summary Generation
      ‚Ä¢	The system calculates tax for each item based on its GST slab (stored in tax Percent).
      ‚Ä¢	A summary of GST amounts by percentage is displayed.
7.	Calculating Final Amount
      ‚Ä¢	Subtotal ‚Üí Apply Discount ‚Üí Calculate GST ‚Üí Add Round-Off ‚Üí Final Total ready for billing.
8.	Creating the Invoice
      ‚Ä¢	When ‚ÄúCreate Invoice‚Äù is clicked, a modal opens to collect:
            ÔÇß	Customer Name
            ÔÇß	Customer Number
            ÔÇß	Payment Method (Cash, Card, UPI)
      ‚Ä¢	The final bill preview is displayed inside the modal.
9.	Saving the Bill
      ‚Ä¢	On submission, the bill is saved in the backend (/api/bills/add-bill) and the cart is cleared.
      ‚Ä¢	The system redirects to the Bills Page for review/printing.

**4.11.4 Inventory Dashboard Metrics Explanation**
